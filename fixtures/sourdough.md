# Sourdough baking notes — starter, hydration, and proofing

Feed the starter 1:1:1 by weight (starter, flour, water) and use it when it doubles and floats.
A 75% hydration dough is forgiving for beginners; push toward 80% once you trust your shaping.
Bulk ferment at room temperature until the dough rises about 50% and shows bubbles on the surface.
Cold-proof the shaped loaf overnight in the fridge to deepen flavor and make scoring easier.
Bake in a preheated Dutch oven at 245°C with the lid on for 20 minutes, then uncovered until deep brown.
A good ear and an open crumb come from steam, tension during shaping, and not over-proofing.
